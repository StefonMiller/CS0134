Gasoline Energy Converter

Gasoline is used to fuel an unfathomable number of commercial and industrial vehicles. To truly put it in perspective, you can relate it to the oil that produces it as well as ethanol, a biofuel sometimes used to power cars.

One barrel of oil produces 19.5 gallons of gas. 1 gallon of gas produces 115,000 BTU while 1 gallon of ethanol produces 75,700 BTU, thus you can relate gas to ethanol by dividing 115,000 by 75,700 to get how many gallons of ethanol equal a gallon of gasoline. 1 gallon of gasoline is equivalent to 4 USD and 20 pounds of CO2 as well. All conversion rates for gallons and liters were taken from CourseWeb. Conversions from Olympic Swimming pool to gallons courtesy of wikipedia

This calculator takes an input in gallons(default), liters, and olympic swimming pools and tells you the equivalent number of oil barrels, pounds of co2, pounds of ethanol, and price.
